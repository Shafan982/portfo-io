Project

Gantry Paint Pro

The Gantry Paint Pro is an innovative automated painting machine developed as our final year project at Government Engineering College Palakkad. This compact system features a miniature gantry design, capable of painting wall sections up to 100 cm high. Utilizing Raspberry Pi and Arduino for precise control, it employs stepper motors and a spray nozzle for accurate paint application. Enhanced with edge detection algorithms, the Gantry Paint Pro effectively avoids obstacles, showcasing our skills in mechatronics and automation. This project reflects our journey from theoretical learning to practical innovation, preparing us for future careers in engineering and technology.

  • Control Architecture: Raspberry Pi for high-level control and Arduino for real-time motor management.
  • Actuation: Stepper motors for accurate positioning and consistent paint application.
  • Spray Mechanism: Calibrated spray nozzle for uniform paint coverage.
  • Edge Detection: Computer vision algorithms to avoid obstacles.
CNC Plotter

We successfully designed and prototyped a 3-axis CNC plotter using Arduino, combining CAD modeling in Fusion 360 with embedded systems programming. This project involved coordinating stepper motors, limit switches, and driver circuitry for precise 2D vector drawing along three axes. I developed a graphical interface for intuitive control, honing skills in part design, microcontroller programming, and hardware-software integration.

  • CAD Design: Modeled in Fusion 360 for precision and mechanical efficiency.
  • Microcontroller Programming: Programmed Arduino for G-code interpretation and motor control.
  • Motor Control: Configured NEMA stepper motors with A4988 drivers for 3-axis motion.
  • Limit Switches: Implemented homing and boundary detection for accurate positioning.
  • Circuit Design: Created a reliable control circuit with power management.
  • Precision Calibration: Fine-tuned motor settings for high-accuracy movement.
